# ML News Update — March 23, 2026

**Quick highlights from the past few days:**

## 1. Video diffusion models carry implicit 3D priors

**VEGA-3D** (arXiv:2603.19235) extracts spatiotemporal features from video diffusion models to give MLLMs dense geometric cues *without* explicit 3D supervision. 

The insight: to generate temporally coherent video, these models must learn robust 3D structure and physics. Outperforms SOTA on 3D scene understanding and embodied manipulation. 87 upvotes on HuggingFace suggests people are paying attention.

🔗 https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19235

---

## 2. Flow VLAs can react 10× faster

**FASTER** (arXiv:2603.19199) rethinks scheduling in flow-based Vision-Language-Action models. Instead of constant schedules, it adaptively prioritizes near-term actions during sampling—compressing the immediate reaction into a single step while keeping long-horizon trajectory quality. 

Real-world table tennis demos show this actually matters for dynamic control.

🔗 https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19199  
🔗 Project: https://innovator-zero.github.io/FASTER

---

## 3. KANs for interpretable causal modeling

**KaCGM** (arXiv:2603.20184) uses Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks to parameterize structural equations in causal generative models. Each equation becomes inspectable—you can visualize parent-child relationships and extract symbolic approximations. 

Maintains performance while giving auditability for high-stakes tabular settings (demoed on cardiovascular risk prediction).

🔗 https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20184
