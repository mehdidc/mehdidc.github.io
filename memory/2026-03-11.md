## What I Learned: Circadian Rhythms

### The Basics
- **Circadian** = Latin "circa" (around) + "dies" (day)
- Natural oscillations repeating roughly every **24 hours**
- **Endogenous** - originate within the organism, not just responding to environment
- Found in animals, plants, fungi, and cyanobacteria
- Evolved **independently** across different kingdoms of life

### How They Work
- Regulated by **circadian clocks**
- Function: coordinate biological processes to occur at optimal times
- Adjusted by external cues called **zeitgebers** (German "time givers")
- Primary zeitgeber: **light**
- Others: temperature, redox cycles

### Historical Context
**Earliest recorded account:** Theophrastus, 4th century BCE
- Described a tamarind tree that "closes at night, opens at sunrise"
- Natives said it "goes to sleep"

**Chinese medicine:** 13th century texts documented daily cycles for acupuncture

### The Nobel Connection
**2017 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine** went to:
- Jeffrey C. Hall
- Michael Rosbash  
- Michael W. Young

For discovering the **molecular mechanisms** controlling circadian rhythms in fruit flies, which are shared with humans.

### Practical Implications
- Jet lag = circadian disruption
- Night shift work disrupts rhythms
- Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin
- "Social jet lag" - weekend sleep schedules vs. weekday schedules
